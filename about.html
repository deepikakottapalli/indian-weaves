About Indian Weaves

A digital guide to India's rich handloom and textile heritage

About The Project

Indian Weaves is a web-based digital guide dedicated to documenting and celebrating India's rich handloom and textile heritage. This platform was built with the vision of making authentic information about Indian weaving traditions, cultural festivals and regional textile arts accessible to everyone — from students and researchers to tourists and textile enthusiasts.


India has one of the richest textile traditions in the world, with each region having its own unique weaving style, patterns and cultural significance. From the golden Banarasi silk of Varanasi to the vibrant Bandhani of Rajasthan, from the delicate Pashmina of Kashmir to the sacred Kasavu of Kerala — every weave carries a story of culture, craftsmanship and celebration. This platform documents these traditions along with the festivals and cultural events that keep these art forms alive.

Our Mission

To preserve and promote India's handloom heritage by creating a comprehensive digital platform that documents weaving traditions, celebrates cultural festivals, highlights regional textile variations and connects people with India's living textile legacy — ensuring these art forms are never forgotten.

Our Vision

To become the most trusted digital encyclopedia of Indian handloom traditions — a platform where every weave tells its story, every festival is celebrated, every artisan gets recognition, and India's textile heritage is preserved for future generations.

Why Handlooms Matter

Cultural Heritage

Indian handlooms are living traditions passed down through generations. Each weave carries centuries of history, art and cultural identity of its region.

Artisan Livelihoods

Over 43 lakh weavers across India depend on handloom weaving for their livelihood. Supporting handlooms means supporting millions of families.

Sustainable Fashion

Handloom textiles are eco-friendly and sustainable. They use natural fibers and traditional processes that have minimal environmental impact.

Global Recognition

Many Indian weaves have received GI tags and UNESCO recognition, establishing them as unique cultural treasures of global importance.
